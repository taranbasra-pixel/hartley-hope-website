About Us

People at the heart of everything we do

Hartley Hope CIC was founded with a simple but powerful belief: everyone deserves a safe, stable home and the right support to build the life they want.

As a Community Interest Company, our surplus is reinvested directly into improving our accommodation and services. We work closely with local authorities, NHS partners, and social care teams to deliver consistent, compassionate support.

What We Offer

Our Services

From move-in to move-on, wrap-around support at every stage of a resident's journey.

🏠

Supported Accommodation

Safe, fully furnished properties tailored for adults who need a stable home alongside structured support to develop daily living skills.

👥

Key Working & Mentoring

Each resident is assigned a dedicated key worker providing regular one-to-one sessions, goal setting, and practical assistance.

📋

Support Planning

Personalised support plans created in collaboration with each resident, their family, and professionals — reviewed regularly.

🎓

Life Skills Development

Cooking, budgeting, tenancy management, and social skills — building capabilities needed for independent living.

🧠

Wellbeing Support

Our team supports residents with mental health needs, working alongside NHS and community services for joined-up care.

🚀

Move-On Support

When residents are ready, we help them access social housing, private renting, or other pathways — a smooth, confident transition.

Our Approach

We believe a good landlord partnership is built on trust, transparency and mutual respect. We treat every property as if it were our own, and we keep you informed every step of the way. Our team is on-hand to deal with any issues quickly — so you never feel left in the dark.

Common Questions

Will my property be well looked after?
Yes. We carry out regular property inspections and maintain a detailed log of the property's condition. Any wear and tear is addressed promptly, and we will always notify you before undertaking any work beyond routine maintenance.
What if something goes wrong with a resident?
Our trained support staff are present regularly and on call. Any incidents are managed by our team — you won't be contacted about resident-related issues. We handle everything through our own safeguarding and support procedures.
Do I need an HMO licence?
It depends on your property and local authority requirements. We'll walk you through exactly what's needed during our initial assessment and can support you through the licensing process if required.
How long are your lease agreements?
We typically agree leases of 3 to 5 years, with the option to renew. We're flexible and will discuss the arrangement that works best for you during our initial conversation.
Is this only for landlords with multiple properties?
Not at all. We work with individual landlords who own just one property, as well as those with larger portfolios. If your property is a good fit, we'd love to hear from you.
